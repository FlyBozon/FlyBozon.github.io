BEng Thesis · Dec 2024 — Dec 2025

Autonomous Drone Navigation in GPS-Denied Environments

An autonomous drone navigation system for GPS-denied environments, developed as part of my engineering thesis at Gdansk University of Technology. The system relies on vision-based localization using semantic segmentation and spatial pattern matching, allowing a drone to estimate its position relative to satellite imagery without GPS.

  • Hybrid architecture: ORB-VO relative localization + semantic segmentation absolute correction
  • Trained U-Net (EfficientNet-B3) for semantic segmentation — mean IoU 0.734, 94.5% pixel accuracy on Landcover.ai
  • Reference map generation via fusion of Geoportal orthophotos with OpenStreetMap topographic data
  • Three matching methods implemented: voting accumulator (4.82px error), RANSAC, and combined approach (58% faster)
  • ORB-VO achieved 0.22% relative drift over 73 km; periodic correction reduced mean error by 78.4%
  • Custom PyQt5 GUI for reference map creation with interactive map viewer
